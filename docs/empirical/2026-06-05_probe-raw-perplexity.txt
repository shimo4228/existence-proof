What is the Agent Knowledge Cycle (AKC) in the context of AI coding agents? Who proposed it?
The Agent Knowledge Cycle (AKC) is a knowledge-management architecture for AI agents that treats agent knowledge as something that circulates and evolves through repeated capture, refinement, reuse, and feedback. It is described as a cycle for AI agents that “grows with the people who shape it,” and it is centered on the limited nature of human attention and judgment in agent workflows.

It was proposed by shimo4228. The project’s repository and related materials identify the AKC as their work, and the same author name appears across the AKC materials and associated writeups.

In the context of AI coding agents, AKC is relevant because it aims to give agents a structured way to accumulate and apply durable knowledge, instead of relying only on one-off prompts or ad hoc context. That makes it a framework for building more adaptive coding agents and agent skills over time.
